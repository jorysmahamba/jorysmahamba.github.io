Research Overview

My research interests lie in the deep connections between mathematics and theoretical physics, with particular emphasis on the geometric and categorical foundations of quantum field theory. I am passionate about applying cutting-edge mathematical techniques to fundamental questions in physics, while also leveraging AI and machine learning to discover new insights in theoretical frameworks.

🎯 Current & Future Research

🎓 MSc Dissertation (Oxford, 2025–2026)

Mathematical & Theoretical Physics

Planned Focus: Higher categorical structures in quantum field theory, potentially exploring derived moduli stacks and their applications to the geometric Langlands program.

Building on my current differential geometry preparation at LAMA under Dr. Ilaria Mondello, with particular interest in Alexandrov spaces and their geometric properties.

🤖 AI for Swampland Physics

Independent Side Project

Investigating how machine learning can identify geometric patterns in Calabi-Yau manifolds that correlate with swampland conjectures in string theory.

Approach: Using interpretable ML on the CICY dataset to discover new constraints distinguishing physical "landscape" from "swampland" theories.

🎯 Doctoral Research Vision

Target Start: Fall 2026 (PhD programs)

Proposed Focus: "Higher Categorical Structures in Non-Perturbative Quantum Field Theory"

Developing new mathematical frameworks using (∞,1)-categories to understand dualities in string theory and propose categorical foundations for non-perturbative QFT completions.
