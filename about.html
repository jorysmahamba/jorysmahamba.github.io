Quick Facts

  • Nationality: French (Franco-Congolese)
  • Languages: French, English (C1), Spanish

I'm Jorys A. Mahamba, a French student in the MSc in Mathematical and Theoretical Physics at the University of Oxford. My passion lies at the intersection of pure mathematics and theoretical physics, driven by the ambition of developing new mathematical frameworks for quantum gravity.

I hold a dual BSc in Mathematics and Physics, having graduated valedictorian from Université Paris-Est Créteil after three years of Classe Préparatoire (PCSI/PC/PC*) in France, where I finished high school at age 15. I hope to work on higher category theory as a foundational tool for non-perturbative quantum field theory for my master's dissertation at Oxford, framing it as a technical precursor to my doctoral project.

My broader research interests include the role of quantum information in spacetime emergence from entanglement, derived algebraic geometry and quantum spacetime, condensed mathematics, and mirror symmetry. I am also pursuing a side project combining AI and string theory for landscape and swampland analysis, using tools from data science to identify geometric patterns across vacua.

Beyond research, I am deeply committed to education and outreach. Under the name "Jorys, The Mathematician", I write accessible blog posts on mathematics and physics and document my academic journey.

At the heart of my journey is a belief that science should be rigorous but human, abstract yet grounded. My work is guided by a deep sense of wonder, a passion for unity across disciplines, and a lifelong commitment to learning.
