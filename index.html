Bridging Topology and Geometry: The Gauss-Bonnet Theorem

September 7, 2025
Geometry Topology

The Gauss-Bonnet theorem stands as a cornerstone of differential geometry, revealing an unexpected and profound relationship between the local curvature of a surface and its global topological properties. At its heart lies a simple yet striking formula: for any smooth, compact, orientable surface X, the integral of the Gaussian curvature K over the entire surface equals 2π times the Euler characteristic χ(X).

Abstract: This post explores the theorem progressing from its one-dimensional analogue for plane curves to its full formulation for smooth surfaces. We establish two critical foundations: the topological invariance of the Euler characteristic through homological methods and Gauss's Theorema Egregium.

This equality bridges two seemingly disparate mathematical realms: the infinitesimal geometry encoded in curvature and the global topology captured by the Euler characteristic. My recent writing sample provides a detailed proof of this theorem, filling gaps often left implicit in standard treatments.
