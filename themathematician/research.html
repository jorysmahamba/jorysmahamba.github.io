The Physics Behind Hopfield Networks: From Ising Models to Neural Computation

Complexity:

The 2024 Nobel Prize in Physics recognized how magnetic materials inspired artificial neural networks. My research survey explores Hopfield networks — systems that store memories like magnets store magnetic states.

Key insight: The same mathematics describing phase transitions in magnets explains how neural networks learn and remember, bridging 1920s physics with 2020s AI.

Mathematical Exploration of Phase Transitions in the Ising Model

Complexity:

The Ising model is a cornerstone of statistical physics that explains how magnets work at the atomic level. My BSc research internship provided a mathematically rigorous exploration of phase transitions — why materials suddenly change properties at critical temperatures.

Key findings: Using advanced probability theory, I proved there's no phase transition in 1D but demonstrated its existence in 2D, bridging abstract mathematics with observable physics.

The Big Questions I'm Tackling

1. What is the true nature of space and time?

Are they fundamental, or do they emerge from something deeper? My work on quantum gravity suggests space and time might be like temperature — useful concepts that arise from more fundamental ingredients.

2. Can mathematics predict new physics?

History says yes! Maxwell's equations predicted radio waves; Dirac's equation predicted antimatter. I'm using advanced mathematics to predict what might lie beyond our current understanding.

3. How can AI accelerate theoretical physics?

Machine learning isn't just for recommendations and chatbots. I'm exploring how AI can help us navigate the vast mathematical landscapes of modern physics.
