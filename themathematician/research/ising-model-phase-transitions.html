Mathematical Exploration of Phase Transitions in the Ising Model

BSc Research Internship • Laboratory of Analysis and Applied Mathematics (LAMA) • UPEC

Statistical Physics Probability Theory Phase Transitions Mathematical Physics

Supervisor: Dr. Paul DARIO • Duration: 6 weeks (April-May 2024)

Executive Summary

This research internship aimed to bridge a pedagogical gap in the teaching of statistical physics by providing a mathematically rigorous exploration of the Ising model. Working under the supervision of Dr. Paul DARIO at LAMA, I investigated the fundamental properties of this cornerstone model in statistical mechanics, focusing on phase transitions in one and two dimensions.

The work demonstrates the absence of phase transitions in 1D and proves their existence in 2D using advanced mathematical techniques rarely presented at the undergraduate level. This research serves as an ambitious pedagogical resource for motivated students seeking to understand the deep connections between mathematics and physics.

Key Contributions

  • Rigorous Mathematical Framework: Developed a formal presentation of the Ising model using measure theory and probability, making abstract concepts accessible to advanced undergraduates.
  • GKS Inequalities: Provided detailed proofs of the Griffiths-Kelly-Sherman inequalities, establishing fundamental correlation properties in the model.
  • Dimensional Analysis: Contrasted the behavior in 1D (no phase transition) with 2D (phase transition exists) through careful mathematical analysis.
  • Pedagogical Innovation: Created a resource that fills the gap between physics intuition and mathematical rigor, suitable for ambitious science education.

Mathematical Framework

The Ising model describes a system of interacting spins on a lattice, where each spin can be in one of two states: +1 (up) or -1 (down). The system's behavior is governed by the Hamiltonian:

$$H(\sigma) = -\sum_{\{i,j\}} \sigma_i \sigma_j$$

where the sum is over nearest-neighbor pairs and $\sigma_i \in \{-1, +1\}$

The probability of a configuration follows the Gibbs measure:

$$\mu_\beta(\sigma) = \frac{e^{-\beta H(\sigma)}}{Z(\beta)}$$

where $\beta = 1/(k_B T)$ is the inverse temperature and $Z(\beta)$ is the partition function

Main Results

1. Correlation Inequalities (GKS)

Theorem (Griffiths-Kelly-Sherman):

For any finite subset $\Lambda \subset \mathbb{Z}^d$ and any $A, B \subseteq \Lambda$:

$$\langle \sigma_A \sigma_B \rangle_\beta^+ \geq \langle \sigma_A \rangle_\beta^+ \langle \sigma_B \rangle_\beta^+$$

This establishes that spins are positively correlated in the ferromagnetic Ising model.

2. One-Dimensional Case

Theorem (Absence of Phase Transition in 1D):

For the 1D Ising model, for all $\beta > 0$:

$$\langle \sigma_0 \rangle_\beta^+ = 0$$

This means there is no spontaneous magnetization at any finite temperature, hence no phase transition in one dimension.

3. Two-Dimensional Case

Theorem (Peierls' Argument):

For the 2D Ising model, there exists a critical inverse temperature $\beta_c > 0$ such that:

  • For $\beta < \beta_c$: $\langle \sigma_0 \rangle_\beta^+ = 0$ (disordered phase)
  • For $\beta > \beta_c$: $\langle \sigma_0 \rangle_\beta^+ > 0$ (ordered phase)

This demonstrates the existence of a phase transition in two dimensions, consistent with experimental observations of ferromagnetic materials.

Methodological Innovation

This work's primary contribution lies in its pedagogical approach. By employing rigorous mathematical proofs typically reserved for graduate-level courses, I aimed to create a resource that:

  • Bridges the gap between physics intuition and mathematical formalism
  • Provides complete, detailed proofs accessible to motivated undergraduates
  • Systematically connects abstract mathematical results to physical phenomena
  • Serves as a model for mathematically ambitious science education

The use of modern AI tools (ChatGPT, Jenni.ai) was judiciously integrated into the research process, demonstrating how these technologies can enhance academic productivity while maintaining rigor.
