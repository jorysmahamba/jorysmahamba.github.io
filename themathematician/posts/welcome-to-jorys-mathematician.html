Welcome to Jorys, The Mathematician

A multicultural mathematician's journey through freedom, knowledge, and the beautiful intersection of rigorous thinking with human experience

June 16, 2025 • 8 min read

Welcome Personal Philosophy Vision

Bonjour, and welcome to what I hope will become a meaningful corner of the internet. My name is Jorys A. Mahamba, and this space — "Jorys, The Mathematician" — represents something I've been dreaming about for years: a place where mathematical rigor meets human curiosity, where abstract theorems dance with lived experience, and where the pursuit of truth becomes an act of liberation.

At 20 years old, I'm French, from a Portuguese born mother and a Congolese father, a student preparing for my MSc in Mathematical and Theoretical Physics at the University of Oxford, in England, and someone who believes deeply that mathematics is not just about solving equations — it's about understanding the very fabric of reality and our place within it.

My Vision for This Space

"Mathematics is the language in which God has written the universe, but it's also a language in which we can write our freedom."

Why "Jorys, The Mathematician"?

Actually, it's nothing more than a reference to one of my favorite musical artists, Tyler, The Creator. I hesitated for long between "The Mathematician", "The Curious Mind", "The Explorer", or other such denominations, but I finally chose the most simple, which is I believe, also all-encompassing in some sense.

This blog is where I bring together all the pieces of who I am: the rigorous thinker who loves the elegance of geometry, and find great comfort in the abstraction of category theory, the dreamer who sees mathematics as a form of emancipation, the 20-year-old who finished high school at 15 and sometimes still feels like he's catching up to his own ambitions...

"Mathematics is not just what I do — it's how I see the world. Every pattern in nature, every symmetry in art, every structure in society can be understood through the lens of mathematical thinking."

What You'll Find Here

This isn't your typical academic blog, nor is it purely personal. It's something in between — a space where I can explore ideas without the constraints of formal publication, where I can make connections between seemingly disparate fields, and where I can share the wonder I feel when mathematics reveals its secrets.

🔢 Mathematics & Physics

From accessible explanations of complex concepts to deep dives into cutting-edge research, I'll share the mathematical ideas that fascinate me. You'll find posts about:

  • The poetry hidden in differential forms and how geometry speaks to the soul
  • Quantum gravity and our quest to understand the fundamental nature of space and time
  • Category theory as the "mathematics of mathematics"
  • The beautiful connections between physics and artificial intelligence

📚 Education & Outreach

I believe knowledge should be shared, not hoarded. You'll find:

  • Explanations that make complex ideas accessible without dumbing them down
  • Thoughts on effective teaching and learning strategies
  • Resources for students navigating advanced mathematics
  • Reflections on democratizing mathematical knowledge

✨ Personal Journey

The human side of mathematics — the struggles, triumphs, and everything in between:

  • Stories from my academic path and what I've learned along the way
  • The challenges and joys of being a young mathematician
  • Cultural reflections and how they inform my work
  • Dreams for the future and what drives me forward

A Different Kind of Mathematics Blog

What makes this space different? It's the recognition that mathematics is fundamentally human. Every theorem was discovered by a person with hopes, fears, and cultural context. Every equation represents someone's attempt to understand something about our universe.

🎯 My Promise to You

I promise to never talk down to you, to always credit the human stories behind mathematical ideas, and to show that mathematics is not just for a select few — it's for anyone curious enough to ask "why?" and patient enough to seek answers.

Whether you're a fellow mathematician, a curious student, someone who "hates math" but loves beautiful ideas, or just someone who believes in the power of knowledge to transform lives — this space is for you.

My Journey So Far

Let me share a bit about the path that brought me here. I finished high school at 15 — not because I was trying to rush through life, but because I was hungry for knowledge and the French system allowed me to accelerate. After three years in the most intensive French scientific program, the Classes Préparatoires aux Grandes Ecoles, I completed dual BSc degrees in Mathematics and Physics as valedictorian at UPEC, all while maintaining my training as a competitive karateka and handball player: I hold a 1st Dan Shotokan karate black belt and won multiple competitions, up to international level, in both handball and karate (I also played badminton up to international level before finishing high-school).

After an exploratory break into the world of AI and data sciences, as I prepare to begin my MSc at Oxford in October 2025, I'm at an exciting crossroads. I'm old enough to have developed my own perspectives on mathematics and physics, but young enough to still feel the wonder of discovery. I'm experienced enough to engage with cutting-edge research, but still remember what it feels like to struggle with new concepts.

This combination — of rigorous training and maintained curiosity, of academic achievement and human empathy — is what I bring to this blog.

Freedom as My North Star

You'll notice the word "freedom" appears often in my writing. It's not just rhetoric — it's the philosophical foundation of everything I do. I see the pursuit of knowledge as fundamentally an act of liberation: liberation from ignorance, from limited perspectives, from fear, from the constraints that others might try to place on our thinking, or even from death...

When I understand a new theorem, when I make a connection between previously separate ideas — I become a little more free. And when I share that knowledge, when I help others see these connections, I'm participating in the collective liberation of human understanding.

What's Coming Next

In the coming months, you can expect:

  • Deep dives into current research: I'll explain my fascination with quantum gravity, string theory, and category theory in accessible terms
  • Educational content: Making complex mathematical ideas accessible to anyone willing to think deeply
  • Personal insights: The joys and challenges of life as a young mathematician

I'll also be sharing my journey as I prepare for Oxford, reflecting on what it means to study mathematical and theoretical physics at one of the world's leading universities, and exploring how this experience shapes my understanding of mathematics and my place in the scientific community.

Join the Journey

This isn't just my blog — it's a conversation. I want to hear from you: your questions, your insights, your own connections between mathematics and life. Whether you're a seasoned researcher or someone who's never thought about mathematics as beautiful, your perspective matters.

You can connect with me through the comments on posts, via email, or on social media. I'm particularly interested in hearing from:

  • Students navigating mathematics or physics and looking for guidance or encouragement
  • Fellow (aspiring) researchers interested in interdisciplinary connections
  • Anyone curious about how mathematical thinking can illuminate other fields
  • Educators looking for new ways to make mathematics engaging and accessible
  • Anyone who believes, like I do, that knowledge is a path to freedom

A Final Thought

"Mathematics is not about numbers, equations, computations, or algorithms: it is about understanding." — William Paul Thurston

Welcome to a space dedicated to understanding — of mathematics, of ourselves, and of the beautiful connections that bind them together.

Thank you for being here. Thank you for being curious. And thank you for joining me on this journey toward greater understanding and freedom.

Avec toute ma gratitude,
Jorys A. Mahamba
