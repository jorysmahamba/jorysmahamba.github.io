The Nobel Prize in Physics 2024: A Celebration of Machine Learning's Foundations in Physics

Under review

J. A. Mahamba
Submitted to: La Gazette des Mathématiciens, Société Mathématique de France

A comprehensive mathematical review exploring the deep connections between Hopfield Networks and the Ising model, demonstrating how statistical physics provides the theoretical foundation for modern neural network architectures. This survey celebrates the 2024 Nobel Prize awarded to John J. Hopfield and Geoffrey E. Hinton for their foundational contributions to machine learning through physics-inspired models.

Mathematical Exploration of Phase Transitions in the Ising Model

J. A. Mahamba
BSc Research Report, LAMA Laboratory, Université Paris XII (2024)
Supervisor: Dr. Paul Dario

A rigorous mathematical treatment of phase transitions in the Ising model using measure theory, correlation inequalities (GKS), and thermodynamic limits. The report demonstrates the absence of phase transitions in one dimension versus their existence in two dimensions, with applications to statistical physics education.
